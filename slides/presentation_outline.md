## Slide Spine

1. Title: Space, Body Image, and Resistance Training Avoidance
2. Why this matters: access to a gym is not the same as access to every training zone
3. Research questions: space, media ideals, negotiation
4. Methods: 32 participant-level survey responses plus 3 semi-structured interviews
5. Sample pattern: many use the gym, few use free weights
6. RQ1: spatial pressure is the clearest mechanism, supported by alpha and Spearman results
7. RQ2: media ideals affect exercise choices, but their link with avoidance is weaker
8. Qualitative explanation: visibility, beginner shame, male-dominated atmosphere
9. RQ3: students negotiate rather than simply withdraw
10. What campuses can do: workshops, semi-private beginner space, functional messaging
11. Limitations: exploratory individual-level evidence, small sample, no causal modelling
12. Conclusion: the core barrier is beginner access in a gendered space

## Delivery Notes

- Keep slides visually simple; do not read every number.
- Use the script as a guide, not a word-for-word obligation.
- The strongest repeated phrase for the presentation: "not lack of interest, but uneven access within the gym."
- Cronbach's alpha, Spearman correlations, and Mann-Whitney comparisons can be mentioned, but only as small-sample exploratory evidence.
- Avoid claiming regression, mediation, prediction, or causal proof.
- Avoid calling the three interviews a focus group.
